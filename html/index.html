Why UI/UX Designing and not Film making?

While I loved filmmaking, I realised it was more of a creative outlet than a career path for me. Filmmaking taught me how to tell stories, think visually, and work under pressure—all of which I now apply to UI/UX design. In UI/UX, I get the same creative satisfaction but with the added excitement of problem-solving and crafting user-centred experiences. It’s a perfect blend of creativity and strategy, which I find truly fulfilling.

What attracted you to UI/UX design?

I was always drawn to storytelling, whether through photography or filmmaking. As I transitioned into UI/UX design, I realised it offered a similar satisfaction. Here, I craft digital experiences that guide users seamlessly through a narrative, solving real-world problems through design. The blend of creativity and logical structure was what hooked me.

Can you give an example of a project you’re proud of in your UI/UX career?

One of my proudest projects was designing a complete user flow and architecture for a real estate ERP system. From conducting user interviews to designing and refining the product, I was deeply involved in every stage. The challenge of simplifying complex workflows while ensuring a smooth user experience was a rewarding experience.
